‚ö†Ô∏è Why This Matters to YOU

You might think, "I'm not a target‚ÄîI'm just a regular person." But cybercriminals don't discriminate. They use automated tools that scan millions of systems looking for vulnerabilities. Your personal computer, your social media accounts, and your online banking are all potential targets.

ü¶† Malware: The Digital Disease

Malware is short for "malicious software." It's any software intentionally designed to cause damage to a computer, server, or network.

ü¶† Viruses

What it does: Attaches to clean files and spreads, infecting files with malicious code.

How it spreads: Email attachments, downloads, USB drives.

Damage: Corrupts or deletes files, slows systems.

üê¥ Trojans

What it does: Disguises itself as legitimate software.

How it spreads: Users unknowingly download it.

Damage: Creates backdoors for attackers.

üîí Ransomware

What it does: Encrypts files and demands payment.

How it spreads: Phishing emails, malicious links.

Damage: Average ransom: $812,000.

üïµÔ∏è Spyware

What it does: Secretly monitors activity.

How it spreads: Bundled with free software.

Damage: Steals passwords and personal data.

üêõ Worms

What it does: Self-replicates across networks.

How it spreads: Automatically through networks.

Damage: Rapidly infects entire networks.

üì± Adware

What it does: Displays unwanted ads.

How it spreads: Bundled with free software.

Damage: Slows systems, tracks behavior.

üìñ Real-World Example: WannaCry Ransomware (2017)

WannaCry infected over 200,000 computers across 150 countries in just 4 days.

  • UK's National Health Service: 19,000+ appointments cancelled
  • FedEx, Nissan, Deutsche Bahn severely affected
  • Estimated damage: $4 billion globally
  • All because organizations hadn't installed a security patch released 2 months earlier

The Lesson: Keep your systems updated!

üé£ Phishing: The Art of Digital Deception

Phishing is a social engineering attack where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to steal sensitive information.

Phishing is the #1 cyber attack method because it exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities.

Why Phishing Works

Phishing attacks manipulate human emotions:

  • Fear: "Your account will be closed!"
  • Urgency: "Act now or lose access!"
  • Curiosity: "You've won a prize!"
  • Trust: "We're from your bank"

Types of Phishing

  • Email Phishing: Mass emails pretending to be from banks or services
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted attacks with personalized information
  • Whaling: Attacks aimed at executives
  • Smishing: Phishing via SMS
  • Vishing: Phishing via phone calls

üö® Red Flags: How to Spot Phishing

  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Too good to be true offers
  • Suspicious sender addresses
  • Generic greetings
  • Requests for personal information
  • Suspicious links or attachments

üé≠ Social Engineering: Hacking the Human

Social Engineering is psychological manipulation to trick people into divulging information.

Common Tactics

  • Pretexting: Creating fabricated scenarios
  • Baiting: Offering something enticing (infected USB drives)
  • Tailgating: Following someone into restricted areas
  • Quid Pro Quo: Offering services for information

üõ°Ô∏è Defense

  • Always verify identity through official channels
  • Question everything suspicious
  • Slow down - don't rush decisions
  • Never share passwords
  • When in doubt, say no

üí• DDoS Attacks

Distributed Denial of Service attacks overwhelm systems with massive traffic, making them unavailable.

How It Works

  1. Attackers infect thousands of computers (botnet)
  2. All infected devices receive commands simultaneously
  3. They flood the target with requests
  4. The server crashes or becomes unresponsive

üìñ GitHub DDoS (2018)

  • Peak traffic: 1.35 terabits/second
  • Largest DDoS attack at the time
  • Recovered in 10 minutes due to protection

üíâ SQL Injection

SQL Injection exploits vulnerabilities in databases by inserting malicious SQL code.

How It Works

Normal query: SELECT * FROM users WHERE username='john' AND password='secret123'

Malicious: username='admin' OR '1'='1'

Result: Bypasses authentication because '1'='1' is always true!

üõ°Ô∏è Prevention

  • Use parameterized queries
  • Validate all user input
  • Use least privilege for database accounts
  • Regular security testing

üéØ Key Takeaways

  • Malware comes in many forms: viruses, trojans, ransomware, spyware, worms, adware
  • Phishing is the #1 attack method - exploits human psychology
  • Social engineering hacks humans, not computers
  • DDoS attacks overwhelm systems with traffic
  • SQL Injection exploits poor coding practices
  • Keep systems updated to prevent attacks
  • Always verify identity before sharing information
  • When something feels off, pause and think
